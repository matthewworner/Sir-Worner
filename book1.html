Meet Wally Worner

The Year: 1565

Long ago, there lived a brave knight named Sir Wallace "Wally" Worner. His name meant "guardian warrior," and he carried a bright yellow shield with a bold red stripe and six red roses.

Wally wasn't alone in his adventures! He had three young squires who were learning to become knights:

Marco - brave and strong
Seraphine - clever and quick
Lola - kind and determined

The Great Fort

Wally lived in Birgu, a small city by the sea.

He guarded Fort St. Angelo, a giant stone castle. It had thick walls to protect everyone.

"We are safe here," Wally told his friends. "These walls are strong!"

Every day, he climbed the high towers to look for danger across the blue water.

The Perilous Voyage

The voyage across the Mediterranean Sea was their first great adventure!

One night, a terrible storm hit. Lightning cracked! Waves crashed! The ship rocked violently.

"I'm scared!" said Lola, holding tight to the mast.

"We'll be okay," Marco said bravely, though he was scared too.

Seraphine noticed something. "Look! The stars are making a pattern through the clouds!"

Wally saw it too. "Captain! Follow those stars! Change course!"

By morning, they sailed into calm waters. There, golden in the sunrise, was Malta!

"We made it!" the children cheered. Their adventure was just beginning.

The Great Siege Begins

Suddenly, BOOM! The enemy cannons fired!

Day after day, cannonballs flew like angry hornets. Walls crumbled. Dust filled the air.

"Stay close!" Wally shouted to Marco, Seraphine, and Lola as they dodged falling stones.

They fought bravely on the walls of Senglea. They poured water on fires and helped carry wounded soldiers to safety.

Even when they were tired and scared, they remembered the woman at Number 38. "Non nobis tantum nati," they whispered.

The Miracle of August 7th

The fighting was fiercest on August 7th. The enemy was climbing the walls!

"Hold the line!" Sir Wally cried, raising his sword.

Just when all hope seemed lost, a shout rang out. Reinforcements had arrived from Mdina! The enemy was retreating!

"It's a miracle!" Seraphine cheered.

Wally looked at the battered walls of Stella House. It was damaged, but still standing. "We did it," he smiled. "Senglea is safe for another day."
